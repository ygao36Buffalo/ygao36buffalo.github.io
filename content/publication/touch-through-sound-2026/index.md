abstract: Touch Through Sound explores ring-mounted auditory pseudo-haptics for material perception. The work investigates how sound feedback from a wearable ring can enrich touch experiences and help users perceive material qualities without requiring bulky haptic hardware. This project contributes to wearable interaction and multisensory interface design by using auditory cues as a lightweight channel for haptic augmentation.
